Nigerian Food

Jollof Rice

Jollof rice is a popular West African dish made from rice cooked in a flavorful tomato sauce with spices. It is often served with meat, fish, or vegetables and is a staple at parties and celebrations.

Pepper Soup

Pepper soup is a spicy West African broth made with local spices and meats like fish or goat. It is valued for its hot, aromatic taste and light, comforting texture.

Egusi

Egusi soup is a rich, thick soup made from ground melon seeds (egusi), often cooked with spinach or bitterleaf, meat, and fish. It is commonly eaten with fufu, pounded yam, or rice in many West African countries.

View

Akara

Akara are deep-fried bean cakes made from ground black-eyed peas, onions, and spices. They are a popular Nigerian snack or breakfast dish, often served with pap or as street food.

View

Moin-Moin

Moin Moin is a steamed bean pudding made from blended black-eyed peas, onions, and spices. It is often served as a side dish with rice or used as a filling for wraps in Nigerian cuisine.

View

Puff-Puff

Puff puff is a sweet, fried dough snack popular in West Africa. The dough is made from flour, sugar, yeast, and water, then deep-fried until golden and fluffy.
